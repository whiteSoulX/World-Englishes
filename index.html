Origins & Evolution

English is a West Germanic language that absorbed the world.

5000 B.C. - Proto-Indo-European

The ancestral root. English belongs to the Germanic branch, making it a cousin to German and Dutch.

450-1100 A.D. - Old English

The language of Beowulf. Germanic tribes (Angles, Saxons) mix with Vikings (Old Norse).

1100-1500 A.D. - Middle English

The Norman Conquest (1066) brings French. English becomes the language of the masses, French for the elite.

1600-Present - Modern English

The Great Vowel Shift, Shakespeare, and colonial expansion spread the language globally.
